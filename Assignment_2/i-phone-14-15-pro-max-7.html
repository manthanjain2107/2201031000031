Kedarnath

Kedarnath Temple one of the twelve

votive stupa of Aviokitehwar.

The temple is located on the Garhwal 

Himalyan range near the Mandakini

river, in the state of Uttarakhand, 

India. Due to extreme weather

conditions, the temple is open to the

general public only between the

months of April and November During

the winters,the vigrah of the temple

is carried down to Ukhimath to be

worshipped for the next six months.

Kedarnath is seen as a homogeneous

form of Shiva, the 'Lord of Kedarkhand'

, the historical name of the region.
